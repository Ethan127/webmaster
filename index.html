Why Telehealth?

Improves the Comfort and Convenience of Healthcare
Being forced to commute to your nearest clinic for the most minor of concerns can be a hassle and even a waste of time. No matter what your issue is or where you currently are, telehealth grants patients nearly instantaneous access to professional medical consulting. All you need is a device, Internet connection, and somewhere to sit back and relax.
Perfect for this Pandemic
With the COVID-19 pandemic still raging across the globe, hospitals and clinics are some of the worst places to travel right now. Patients with all kinds of illnesses and diseases, including coronavirus, constantly enter the building. Especially for those who are vulnerable or immunocompromised, it is crucial that these patients are able to access healthcare from a risk-free environment. Telehealth allows patients to get help from the safest location: their own home.
Physicians Make Better Judgments
Many factors can be the culprit behind a person's health issues. Even something as simple as the location where the patient is being assessed can lead to drastically different results. For example, allergies that can be triggered by specific conditions found in an environment the patient is exposed to. When a medical professional is able to contact you while you are at home or at work, they will be able to identify potential causes for concern in the environments you spend the largest part of your life in.
Ensures Everyone Receives the Care They Need
Communicating with your physician online isn't just convenient for you. It diverts patients with minor issues away from hospitals and clinics, allowing those with life-threatening conditions to have easy access to the in-person healthcare they may require. A telehealth system can also accomodate more patients than a traditional medical center, expanding the acccessibility of healthcare for patients all across the board.
